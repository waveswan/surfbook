## Expectations Of A Judge

It is key to understanding how you fit into the
bigger picture of a competition. Let us examine
what the expectations of you are from the
perspective of the coaches, participants, parents
and spectators, administrators, beach marshals
and commentators of surfing.

Coaches
A coach in surfing wants to maximise their team
or participant’s chances of winning in an even
and fair competitive environment. They expect you
to be well prepared, to communicate with them
pre-competition or during competition (where
appropriate), to explain decisions comprehensively,
to be confident and consistent and to work with
them cooperatively in a professional manner. As an
entry-level judge, you will not directly communicate
on a contest site with a coach; the head judge will
complete all explanations and communications with
others. If you were to discuss scores with a coach,
ensure you are open to their opinions and ideas but
stand by your decisions.

Participants
Participants want to enjoy surfing events. This
puts fair judging and conduct at the forefront. They
expect you to know the rules and criteria better
than they do and apply them correctly. Participants
also expect you to be in control, to act ethically
and be reliable with your decisions, to treat them
with respect, remind them of the rules where
appropriate and to take your responsibilities as a
judge seriously.
Parents/Spectators
The families, parents and caregivers of the people
you are judging should be a positive influence.
This involves keeping the consistency of the
scale, communicating and explaining decisions
and helping to make sure all participants are
safe. Spectators expect you to be fair, consistent,
confident and calm.

Administrators
Surfing administrators want you to enforce the
rules of surfing correctly, to present yourself
professionally and with pride, to be a positive and
enthusiastic face of the sport, and to encourage
fair play and enjoyment of the sport.

Beach Marshal and Commentator
Beach marshals and commentators are often
required to observe and concentrate for long
periods of time. These positions are involved
in time keeping and keeping everyone updated
whilst at the event. They are required to keep high
concentration levels for extended periods of time
and also have a high attention to detail.

## Personal Attributes Of A Judge

Professionalism
It is important that you always approach your
judging role with professionalism. You are a public
figure in your sport, and how you conduct yourself
goes a long way to promoting your sport, as well
as encouraging respect for your role.

Behaviour
Your behaviour should be courteous and respectful.
This involves always speaking clearly, confidently,
and using appropriate language. You should treat

people as you would like them to behave towards
you. And remember, to the participants, coaches,
parents and spectators of your sport, this event
that you are judging at is the most important one
for the day.

Presentation
It is important that you present yourself in a
professional manner. This includes wearing
the correct gear and having a generally neat
appearance.

Integrity
You should always aim to be trustworthy, honest
and unbiased, take your role seriously, and be
well prepared for your role. This means both
physically and mentally. If there are areas of skill
and knowledge that you are lacking, you should
seek further training or education to improve your
skills. This will improve your confidence within the
decision-making process. These are important
ways you can act with integrity. They will help
you earn the respect of participants, coaches,
administrators and your judging colleagues.

Conflicts of Interest
Judges are responsible for their actions and should
avoid any situation that may lead to a conflict of
interest. Where possible, try to avoid officiating
your own children or family members, as others
may perceive a bias. If there is no other option
but to officiate your own child or family member,
ensure that you officiate as you would for any other
competition. If you are officiating in a competition
where you may be faced with a conflict of interest,
judge each wave and call on its merits and put
aside any interest or involvement in the outcome of
the contest.

Interactions
Always stay calm and confident when judging
whilst also making sure all interactions are
courteous and professional. Judge every call on its
merits and be prepared to explain your decisions.
You must remember that you are human, and there
will be times when you do not always get it right.
Be open to other views and opinions from the
remaining judging panel or head judge.
