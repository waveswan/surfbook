## Why Reflect On Your Judging?

Judges can enhance their skills in a variety of
ways, including direct experience over time and
the observation of other judges. This is, however,
limited to the use of the experience, and whether
it is being reflected upon to promote learning and
developing judging ability. Knowing what methods
are going to have the most value for positive and
beneficial outcomes is where the use of reflection
can aid in a judge developing themselves in their
profession.

## Reflective Practice

Reflection is a self-learning process. It can
enhance a judge’s ability to look at what they are
doing well, what may need some improvement and
to identify and respond to areas they may need to
work on in the judging environment. This practice
can be used on past events, but also on the go
during an activity or judging session. By integrating
reflection into a judging practice, a judge can make
refinements and adapt judging strategies for any
context and situation they will find themselves in.

How do you rate yourself in those areas that
make up a good official?
Look at these statements, and think about your
knowledge, skills and confidence in each of the
areas before you respond.
1. I have a good understanding and knowledge of
the rules of my sport.
2. I understand my officiating role, and how it fits in
with other officials during competition.
3. I understand the legal responsibilities of my
officiating role.
4. I understand that I am a role model and
ambassador for my sport
5. I understand the expectations on me to act
ethically and impartially.

## Discussions

Discussions allow judges to reflect on their own
practices as well as be exposed to different
approaches other judges are using. This can
stimulate discussion within a team or business
to share a variety of experiences and gain more
insights, solutions and experiment with their own
judging, more so than doing this independently.
Incorporating this into any judging community can
have additional advantages to individual judges
reflecting on their own practice, especially with
different levels and judging experiences in a group.
Below is a list of professionals who these types of
discussions can be had:
• Head Judge
• ISA - Surfing’s governing body.
• Technical & Contest Directors
• Experienced Judges
