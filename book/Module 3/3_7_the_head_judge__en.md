## The Head Judge

The quality and credibility of the contest is
dependent on the quality of the judging. The
general perception ‘on the beach’ has to be that the
contest has reached a fair result on the day and is
dependent on performances.
That is, that the surfers have surfed to, and the
judges have applied “The Judging Criteria” to all
aspects of the surfer’s performances in every heat
and throughout the competition.
The Head Judge’s main responsibilities are to
ensure consistency in scoring, eliminate obvious
discrepancies and to advise the judges of
conditions throughout the day that may affect their
scale.
The Head Judge cannot over-rule a judging panels’
decision concerning interference or wave scores
unless the majority of judges did not see the
incident or wave; however he/ she can act as an
extra judge on interference calls (e.g. with a five
man panel, if two judges mark interference then
the Head Judge may also mark it and it will then be
counted as a majority).
The Qualities needed of a Head Judge are
numerous and reflect generally on the entire panel.
Each Head Judge has a different Head judging
style, and this is a reflection of his/ her personality
and people management skills
To get the best performance for the judging panel
the head Judge needs to ‘manage’ the team, this
involves instinctive people management skills.
The best judging performance comes from judges
who are confident and who can concentrate
throughout the day, achieving these conditions
in the judging tower is the main role of the Head
Judge.

## The Head Judge And Interaction

## With The Judging Panel

In order to succeed, each individual member
of the judging panel including the Head Judge,
must cooperate and act as an effective team.
Each team member must encourage and
support the others to reach a good judging
outcome.

## The Role Of A Head Judge

The Head Judge’s role is to organise, supervise
and instruct the panel of judges so that they can
meet their responsibilities fairly, promptly and
efficiently.
Being a head judge is more than being a good
judge, having the necessary people management
skills will always get the best from your panel.
There are many personality types within a panel
and the working environment around a surfing
contest and this needs to be considered in every
aspect of the working day.
It is very important that Head Judges serve as a
positive role model for aspiring judges.
The behaviour and body language of the head
judge sets the tone for the entire event. How
seriously and how conscientiously a Head Judge
behaves impacts and has a direct bearing on the
whole panel.
Whether it is a world championship tour event, or a
local club contest the judging at the current event
will impact on your reputation as a person and
Head Judge.
It is important that each Head Judge is very mindful
of a positive and professional influence of everyone
and your surroundings.
It is important that Head Judges are completely
aware of duty of care and safety aspects of the
competition and work environments

Give value to your judges.
Judging a surfing competition is the hardest job on
the beach it is extremely tough on the individual as
they are expected to perform to a high standard
for long periods of time in sometimes very difficult
conditions.
Judges are “invisible”, in the same way that a
referee or umpire is and is seemingly only noticed
when he or she or collectively the panel make a
perceived mistake.
The Head Judge can add value to the judges’
esteem by listening to their opinions and praising
their contributions regardless of how many
perceived mistakes have been made.
The necessary skills and efforts used by the judges
every judging day are very rarely appreciated or
understood and it is very important that the Head
Judge supports and appreciates these efforts from
every judge on the panel.
The judges work as a team and support one
another to reach a fair and good judging outcome.

Know the rule book and the updated judging
concepts
Good Head Judges not only know the procedures
of a surf contest they can anticipate a situation
before it develops. This comes with experience and
education.
It is essential to know how the rule book applies to
each new situation. These days there are constant
rule changes.
It is of the paramount of importance that the Head
Judge is completely up to date with current rule
changes and with new judging principles and
concepts.

Be open and honest and encourage this from
your judging team
The reason we have panels of three, four or five
sitting judges is because of the subjective nature
of judging our sport.
If every judge has the same score for every wave
then there would be no need to have a panel.
Encourage your judges to decide for themselves
what score to put down with careful consideration
of the criteria and the scoring scale.
It is important to encourage the judges to have
their own opinion but, it is just as important that
each judge combines works as a united team and
appreciates the opinion of his judging peers and
Head judge.

Take charge
There is nothing worse than a team with no
direction or leadership.
A good Head Judge will lead from the start and
show professionalism and understanding of the
judge’s decisions.

Good preparation is essential; it is difficult to
organise a pre event judging meeting if you are still
drawing up a roster.
Take charge in all panel discussions to avoid
disorder and time-wasting diversions and also to
assure that everyone on the panel contributes to
each decision.
There are many roles that extend from being
the Head Judge this involves being a mediator,
moderator, motivator, and manager.
Mediator – Mediating between judge’s opinions
giving value to all.
Moderator – Moderating large scoring spreads
using known techniques or technology.
Motivator – Motivating individual judges and or
the panel to reach the best judging outcomes
Manager – Managing all aspects of the
operations and communication with competition
staff and athletes

## The Head Judge Responsibilities For

## The Competition

Before the event (In consultation with the Tour
manager and or Event Organisers.)
At most professional surfing competitions, the
Head Judge usually will confirm the day rates and
expenses available for each judge.
The Head Judge will consult on the selection of the
judging panel for the event and be the main point
of contact for the judges.

Site inspection and the Competition Podium
Establish that all the necessary items are
organised, such as water & food for the contest
day and appropriate shelter and amenities for all
of the Judging and Officiating Team, this includes
Judges, Priority judges, Spotters, Replay Camera
operators, Scoring and Replay officials. Assemble
clipboards, judges’ sheets, chairs etc.
Double check the judging podium and working
environment for any perceived problems prior to
the event start.
Ensure that an appropriate view of the surf break
with little or no sight blockages.
The competition day
Arrive early for a briefing with the Tour Manager,
Contest Director and Event Organisers to
discuss if the conditions are appropriate to start
the competition and to establish heat times and
schedule for the day.
The Head judge will establish the contest area and
locate competition area flags and in consultation
with the Priority Judge, the position of the priority
buoys and or the visual aspects of the PTO
(Primary Take off Zone)
The Head judge will also establish what are the
surfing conditions and which Interference rules will
apply.
This information is an integral part of a pre-contest
briefing that will be delivered to the Beach Marshall
who will inform all competitors at the time of check-
in.
The early pre-competition briefing is scheduled
for the Judging panel which includes spotters and
Priority judges.
The scoring scale will be discussed prior to the
first heat and establishes what will be a good/
excellent performance in the current conditions and
for the appropriate age divisions that are surfing.
The Head judge will also assign each judge to
various heats using a roster system.
Operates the Head Judges computer terminal

During the competition
Make sure every wave is scored.
Help the judges establish an appropriate scale
during the first heats of the day and any new age
division or after any significant changes in surf
conditions.
Assists Scoring Judges on technical matters and
answer questions relating to judging (e.g. criteria,
rules, interferences, scale, heat timing, wave
counts, missed waves, average scores, use of
computers, etc)
Ensure the Key words and concepts of the Judging
Criteria are adhered to.
Ensures that the Priority Judge has the necessary
support when needed

Consults with the Priority Judge on the location of
the priority buoy or the location of the Primary take-
off zone as this can be affected by conditions such
as tide and swell.
Ensure conversation is minimized and
concentration is maximized.
Will delegate responsibility when required, usually
to the next most senior judge, to ensure that every
heat is properly overseen.
Advise on the Rule Book if needed by judges.
Acts as the sole representative for all judges with
respect to communicating with other contest
officials, competitors and the press.
Discusses official protests made by competitors at
the end of the round or at the end of the day with
the panel and or competitors.
Reports interference calls to the Contest Director,
Tabulator and Announcer.
Directs the Announcer and Beach Marshall to
inform competitors on various situations during a
heat.
Remain in contact with Contest Director regarding
conditions.
Advise Beach Marshall of any changes to judging
conditions.

After the competition day
Hold a meeting with the judging panel and
competition officials if required.
Handles any complaints and disciplinary action in
relation to the panel.
Debrief contest director on the day’s events.
Assesses the performance of the Scoring Panel by
collating and analysing information and statistics in
regard to individual judging performances.
