## Module 3: Judging

## The Judging Criteria

The key words of the shortboard criteria need to
be considered by the judges when scoring a
surfer’s ride.
The current Shortboard criteria is as follows:
Commitment and Degree of Difficulty
Innovative and Progressive manoeuvres
Combination of Major manoeuvres
Variety of manoeuvres
Speed, Power and Flow
Please note:
It’s important to note that the emphasis of certain
elements is contingent upon the location and
the conditions on the day, as well as changes of
conditions during the day.
Judges must use the key words of the judging
criteria when deliberating on a score for a
competitor’s ride.
The key words showcase exactly what constitutes
good to excellent surfing. It is important that the
Judges, surfers and spectators know exactly the
points on which the competitors are going to be
judged.

Please note:
Judges should consider the skill level and the age
division of the surfers they are scoring. The skill
level and age of the competitors will have a bearing
on some of the scoring areas of the criteria that are
appropriate to that division.

## Commitment And Degree Of

## Difficulty

To perform manoeuvres that will be rewarded
highly by the judges, the surfer must commit to
performing manoeuvres which have a high degree
of difficulty and risk in the critical sections with
control.
The critical section of the wave is the pocket
(closest to the curl). The criteria describe the
position the manoeuvres should be performed on
the wave to score maximum points. The degree of
commitment and the risk involved in performing
close to the breaking parts of a wave or curl, is the
reason it scores highly.
There is less emphasis put on wave size in small
to medium conditions as the best waves may not
necessarily be the biggest.
High-risk manoeuvres executed successfully
= high reward in scoring.
Please note:
In competitions with big wave conditions the most
important part of the criteria would be the size of
the wave and the intensity of the sections.
In renown tube locations the most important part of
the criteria the intensity of the sections and deep
critical tube riding.
The surfer, who is prepared to catch the biggest
wave or gets the longest and deepest tube rides,
shows the greatest commitment and has a higher
degree of difficulty.

## Innovative And Progressive

The words “innovative” and “progressive” in the
current criteria allows the surfers to be more
expressive with more dynamic and futuristic
manoeuvres.
Innovative manoeuvres can be classified as
functional tail slides, carving 360°s, power slides
to reverse 180 to 360 degrees, technical aerials
with different grabs and aerial rotations and flips.
Innovative and progressive major manoeuvres,
executed with speed, power and commitment
become more impressive when performed in the
critical sections of the wave.
Aerials are more impressive when performed in the
critical sections of the wave above and beyond the
lip of the wave with maximum height.

## Combination Of Major Manoeuvres

Combinations of major manoeuvres are important
when considering very good and excellent
scores. A clean and controlled transition between
major manoeuvres is crucial to a high scoring
combination.
Completing a combination of major manoeuvres
with elements of progression, speed and power
of the criteria should be rewarded highly as this
performance has an unusually high degree of
difficulty. Performing the combination on the first
section of a wave is also considered to be of a
higher scoring value.

## Variety Of Manoeuvres

The important aspect of this part of the criteria
is the words “variety of manoeuvres”. A surfer is
required to surf with a variety of manoeuvres on the

waves as this performance has a high degree of
difficulty and will be taken into consideration when
rewarding points.

## Speed, Power And Flow

Speed and power will become more apparent
when performed in the critical section of the wave.
Generally, the greater the speed the more powerful
the manoeuvre will be when performed.
Flow has been included in the new criteria and
relates to how the surfer links turns together on the
wave and how the manoeuvres are functional in
regard to where it’s being performed.
A surfer that is flowing with the wave will look
as though he/she is reading the wave well and
performing the manoeuvres that are suitable to the
sections.
Innovative/progressive surfing as well as the
combination and variety of repertoire (of major
manoeuvres) will be taken into consideration when
rewarding points for a surfers’ performance.
Please note:
Judges should always consider the critical sections
of the wave.
The critical section is the pocket or curl which is
the part of the wave that is closest to the breaking
part of the wave.
All manoeuvres should be performed in the critical
sections to score maximum points.
The first section of the wave is usually considered
to have a higher scoring potential and is regarded
by the judges as an important critical section
because the surfer is risking the entire scoring
potential of the wave.

## Judging Tip

When scoring look for visual references and ask
yourself:
• Did the surfer perform manoeuvres in the
critical sections of the wave?
• Where the manoeuvres performed with speed
and power, did you see large volumes of spray?
• Did any of the manoeuvres contain elements of
variation and or progression?
