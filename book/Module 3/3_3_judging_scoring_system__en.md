## Judging Scoring System

THE POINT SCORING SYSTEM IS 0 – 10, BROKEN INTO ONE-TENTH INCREMENTS.
Use the following five scoring categories: The following descriptions are basic examples.
Poor Ride No manoeuvres or minor manoeuvre with little or no control
Fair Ride Minor manoeuvre or basic manoeuvre with control
Average Ride Basic manoeuvres 1 major and minors with control
Good Ride Radical controlled major manoeuvres speed and power
Excellent Ride Radical controlled major manoeuvres with speed & power with most other
elements of the key words in the criteria
Start scoring a heat with whole and half numbers this will automatically open up your judging
scale for the heat. A clearly defined and open scoring scale will be an advantage as the more
waves are ridden throughout the heat.

## Basic Scale

A judge would normally start a heat by using whole and halves, restricting the range of possible scores in
the entire scale to the following 20 scores:
0.5 1.0 1.5 2.0 2.5 3.0 3.5 4.0 4.5 5.0
5.5 6.0 6.5 7.0 7.5 8.0 8.5 9.0 9.5 10.0

## 2Nd Scale (.2,.3,.7,.8)

Use these decimals once you have scores either side of the whole and half numbers. Use of these
numbers still allows a clear separation between the scores.
