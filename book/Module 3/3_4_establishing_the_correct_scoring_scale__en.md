## Establishing The Correct

## Scoring Scale

It is important to use the full scale when judging.
Take into consideration that when using the full
judging scale, a judge must provide participants
with consistently correct outcomes. Judges must
make the most of a full scale, adjusting their
judging dependent on the age group, level of
competition and to keep consistency across their
heat.
Using the full scoring scale:
• Decreases the amount of ties that occur in
heats.
• Makes the tabulator’s job more efficient.
• Creates fewer judging errors and participant
frustration.
• Minimises inconsistences as participants can
see the full range of scores are being used.

Establishing the scoring scale for different
conditions and age divisions
A good judge will always utilise the full scale
available (from 0.1–10.0) and will re-evaluate the
scale for:

Different age divisions
Taking into account the competency level of each
specific divisions by:
• Increasing the scoring scale for younger boys
and girls, and; older age divisions for men and
women.
• Decrease the scoring scale for juniors, open
divisions and all pro divisions.

Different surf conditions
• Increase the scale if conditions are bad or
deteriorating.
• Decrease the scale for improving or perfect
conditions.

## Judging Tip

Use the full scoring scale. Experience will help
overcome the tendency to score in a narrow
range. Ask your head judge and/or a senior
experienced judge to help in establishing the
correct scoring scale appropriate to the age of
the competitions and conditions.

Further to the above:
• Consider the competency level of each specific
male and female age divisions by increasing the
upper range scale for very young competitors,
older age divisions and lesser skilled divisions.
• Alternatively, a judge should decrease the
scale for Pro Juniors, Open Men’s and any
professional competitions.
• Consider increasing the upper range if
conditions are bad or are deteriorating because
it is much harder to surf well in these types of
conditions. Decrease the scale for improving or
perfect conditions as it should be easier to surf
in good surf conditions.
