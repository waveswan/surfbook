## The Judging Panel

The standard judging panel for a regional or
higher level event consists of the following
members in its basic structure
6 or 7 Scoring Judges
1 Head Judge

For small events:
A minimum of 4 scoring Judges
1 Head Judge
This smaller panel can be used how-ever it is NOT
recommended.
In order to succeed, the individual members of
the judging panel including the Head Judge,
cooperate as an effective team. Each member must
encourage and support the others to reach a good
judging outcome.

## Scoring Judge

The role of the judge is to compare and
evaluate each surfer’s performance against one
another by applying the basic judging criteria
consistently, accurately and fairly.

## The Duties Of A Judge Are

## As Follows

Judge heats according to the roster for the
entire day
Score each wave ridden in a heat using the
scale of 0.1 to 10.0
Record scores on the score sheets or computer
as provided

Complete all the details on score sheets
Rule on any interference situation
Call competitor singlet colours as they are
“up and riding”
Provide wave counts and other information when
required by the Head Judge or other judges
Undertake various duties assigned by the Head
Judge e.g. acting as the Head Judge when
they’ve left the judging podium for any reason
Provide input to pre and post contest briefing’s
under direction of the Head Judge
Have a thorough knowledge of the rule book

## Judging Tip

A strong judge will always remember the
words below and implement each aspect when
deliberating on a surfer’s performance and score.
• EVALUATE – The surfer’s performance on
the wave.
• COMPARE – Each ride to one another, both
with an individual competitor and between
competitors.
• SEPARATE – Use the full scoring scale to
clearly separate the score for each surfer’s
ride on your score sheet.

## A Good Judge Should Possess The

## Following Qualities

Confidence
Concentration
Experience
Recall

Confidence
Judges, who believe they have what it takes to
do the job properly, usually do. A confident judge
has no inclination to check individual scores of the
judges seated next to them.
Those who lack confidence, fool themselves into
making more errors than usual. The inexperienced
judges on the panel usually receive positive
feedback from the Head Judge and other more
experience judges, providing reassurance.
To assist your confidence you should:
Have thorough knowledge of the rule book and
interference rules
Don’t deliberate too long on making your call –
make it quickly and clearly
Stand by your scores and any interference calls
you make. How-ever it is important to have an
open mind when listening to feedback from the
Head Judge or senior judges.

Concentration
There are many factors that contribute to the
lapsing of a judge’s concentration:
Physical fatigue, the result of not getting enough
sleep the night before an event
Mental fatigue, particularly common towards the
end of the day
Distractions caused by other people
Eating, drinking or talking during heats
Poor judging conditions
A sudden change in conditions or a change in
surfing standard
Difficult judging situations e.g. two or more
surfers riding at the same time, multiple
interferences and non-competitors in the line-up
Boredom e.g. especially during long contests
which are held in poor conditions

Tendency to lose concentration is one reason why
judges work in a panel. Although you should not
rely on others to concentrate for you, having the
other judges on the panel can compensate for
some of your loss of concentration.
To help improve your concentration:
Make sure you get a good night’s sleep
Eat and drink prior to judging
Make yourself comfortable
Do not talk unnecessarily during heats
Focus on the line-up and the competitors

Experience
The best judges are usually those who have judged
many competitions, in a variety of conditions and
formats at various levels. Always judge with an
open mind and listen to the advice of your Head
Judge and senior judges on the panel. Try to gain
more experience by judging at as many events as
possible, no matter how small.

Recall
The ability to recall previous waves ridden,
particularly the best waves and what scores they
were given. Your concentration has a direct bearing
on your ability to recall waves and is vital if you are
to maintain the consistency of your scale.
To bring greater efficiency to your judging you can:
Learn from experience and with proper
concentration, develop a method of judging
where you can find the right rhythm and then
good judging becomes clearer and easier
