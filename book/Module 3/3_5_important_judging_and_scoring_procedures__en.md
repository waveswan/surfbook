## Important Judging And Scoring

## Procedures:

1. Start the heat using whole and half points as
much as possible
This automatically opens up your judging scale
and allows for clear separation of your scores. As
the heat progresses you will need to use smaller
point increments. This is allowed only when both
the whole number and 0.5 increments above and
below have been used i.e. a 6.2 may not be given
until a 6.0 and 6.5 have already been used on your
score sheet in that heat.
The experienced judge uses these decimals
effectively to separate rides that were of marginal
difference in quality. In these cases, to give a 6.2,
the judge must be satisfied that that the ride was
better than a 6.0 but not as good as a 6.5 given
earlier.

2. The score given for the first ride or rides will
set the scale for the remainder of the heat
The overall consistency of the judging scale
depends on each judge starting the heat with the
same reference point/score.
After the first quality ride has been completed the
Head Judge will assist each judge starting with
similar scores and that the general opinion of the
panel e.g. the first ride is considered a “Good ride”
and that all scores are within a point of each other.

3. Compare every individual wave score from
start to finish of the heat, keeping in mind
important wave scores to compare against
The most important scores to remember and
compare back to are:
The first wave of the heat
The current highest wave score of the heat
The current highest wave score that the judge
has scored for the day
Please note: this only applies if the age division or
conditions have not changed.
Quality judges will clearly remember:
The best wave from each surfer
The first ride of each surfer
The second best ride of each surfer

A good judge is able to memorise the rides and
scores of each surfers performance.

4. If any part of the ride is not seen, contact the
Head Judge immediately.
The judge should write an “M” in the scoring box
if they have missed any part of a surfers ride. The
Head judge or tabulator will give you an average
score based on all of the other judges scores.

5. Usually a judge will miss either the start or
finish of a surfers ride
These two aspects of the surfers ride can have
a huge bearing on the score. Do not copy or
guess! It is better to get a fair average from the
Head Judge, who will take into account his/hers
individual opinion as well as the opinion and scores
of the other judges on the panel.

6. Ignore the progressive outcome of the heat
and concentrate on comparing the individual
waves
Comparing and remembering the surfer’s
performance to the judging criteria on each wave is
an important aspect of being a good judge.

7. Judging simultaneous rides
Watch the entire sequence of ridden waves before
scoring any wave by identifying the colours in their
correct order.
1st blue, 2nd red, 3rd yellow and associate
these colours with a word from the scale
descriptions that reflects the quality of each of
the rides. 1st was blue “an excellent ride”
2nd was red “a poor ride” 3rd was yellow
“a good ride”.

## Judging Tip

It is vital that the judges reward the surfing
performance of the ride on the wave and NOT
the quality of the wave. This can lead to over-
scoring by inexperienced judges.
