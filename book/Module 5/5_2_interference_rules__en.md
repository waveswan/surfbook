## Interference Rules

Interferences and situations are usually over in
one or two seconds; it is this reason why when
you are judging, you need to remain focused and
concentrate on the surfers in each heat.
The interference rule states:
Reminder: Right Of Way (ROW) means having
the inside position on a wave.
The surfer deemed to have Right Of Way (ROW)
has an unconditional right of way for the entire
duration of that ride. Interference will be called
if a fellow competitor has hindered the scoring
potential of that surfer deemed to have right
of way.
The individual judge determines whether the surfer
with the ROW has been hindered in their scoring
potential by considering the key points in order
below:
1. Has the scoring potential been hindered?
Was there interference or not?
2. Who has inside position? Which surfer has
ROW? In most cases, the inside surfer has
unconditional ROW.
3. What type of interference? What rule reflects
the infringement? E.g. drop-in, snaking,
paddling, breaking down a section, excessive
hassling.
If you are unsure of the rule relating to a particular
situation, speak immediately with the head judge.
Do not be influenced by the judge next to you.

## 1.Point Break

## (Single Direction Wave)

The inside surfer has unconditional ROW for the
duration of that wave.

## 2. Single Peak

## (Left & Right Breaking Wave)

The surfer considered to have the inside
position at the initial point of take-off has ROW
in the direction they choose.
A second surfer may surf in the opposite direction
on the same wave. The surfers may not cross paths
to gain the opposite side of the peak unless, in the
majority of the judges’ opinion, they do so without
hindering the scoring potential of the inside surfer.

## 3.Beach Break

## (Multiple Random Peaks):

Two separate peaks that eventually meet the
first surfer to their feet has unconditional ROW.
If two surfers stand at the same time:
• There will be no penalty if they both give way
by cutting back or kicking out so that neither is
hindered.
• When judging a heat, if surfers cross paths,
collide or hinder one another, you will penalise
the surfer who has been the aggressor at the
point of contact by marking down that ride.
• A double interference will be called if neither
surfer gives way by cutting back or kicking
out and both share responsibility for the
confrontation.

## Snaking

The judge is able to penalise a surfer when:
• The surfer who is farthest inside at the initial
point of take-off and has established wave
possession is entitled to that wave for the
duration of the ride even if another surfer takes
off behind them. The judges will not penalise
the surfer because they have right of way even
though they are in front.
• If, in the opinion of the judges, the second surfer
has interfered with (snaked) the original surfer
with right of way by causing a loss of scoring
potential, an interference may be called on the
second surfer, even though they were behind the
first when the penalty was called.
• If the second surfer has not hindered the original
surfer with right of way, the judges may choose not
to penalise them and will score both surfers’ rides.
The above situations only apply to multiple surfer
heats or one-on-one in non-priority situations.

## Paddling Interference

The judge is able to penalise a surfer when:
• In 3 and 4 person heats or non-priority one-
on-one situations, another surfer paddling for
the same wave should not excessively hinder a
surfer who has inside position or ROW.
• Positioning at the correct point of take-off for a
wave is an integral part of surfing skill and each
competitor must be allowed to reach this chosen
point unhindered.
Paddling interference tactics in the contest area
can be, but will not be restricted to:
• Blocking the direct pathway of an opponent to
the take-off position by paddling across their
line, by taking and holding the natural inside
paddling position.
• The offending surfer contacts or forces the
inside surfer to change their line while paddling
to catch the wave causing loss of scoring
potential.
• The offending surfer obviously causes a section
to break down in front of the inside surfer, which
would not normally have done so. Causes loss of
scoring potential of the surfer with right of way.
• When a surfer is put in a position while paddling

out that they cannot get out of the way of and
a collision occurs between a competitor who
is riding the wave, it is up to a majority of the
judges to call an interference, whether it is
felt to be accidental or not. This is unless it is
felt that the competitor who is riding the wave
contributed to the collision by selecting an
unreasonable and aggressive line to intentionally
collide with the surfer who is paddling out.

## Inteference Penal Ties

• If the majority of the judges call an interference,
then that wave will be recorded as a zero score.
o This applies to a riding interference or a paddling
interference where the surfer catches that wave.
o If it is the best two waves being counted, the
second highest scoring wave of the offending
surfer will be halved and counted in their final
tally on each of the judging sheets and the
master tally sheet.
• If a surfer has less than the required minimum
scoring rides and receives interference, they will
be scored on one less wave i.e. One wave and
the best two counts then their one wave will be
scored as zero. Until the surfer catches another
wave, then the 50% deduction rule will apply.
• If any surfer incurs two or more interference
penalties, they must immediately leave the
competition area.
• The scoring penalty for a surfer with two
interferences is: Their best and second best
scoring rides will be halved (50%) and counted
in their total.
• The specific ride/s where the surfer has
interfered will be counted as a zero scoring ride.
• A majority of the panel must record an
interference for the interference penalty to apply.
The head judge can be included to achieve a
majority decision.
• The rider who is interfered with will be allowed
an additional wave beyond the wave maximum
limit and within the prescribed time limit.
• The exception to this is a double interference
where neither surfer gets an extra wave.
• An extra wave or heat delay as decided by
the Head Judge at the time will also apply to
interference from water photographers, water
security personnel or other outside interference.
