## Module 5: Competition Rules

## Sirens

At the start of the heat there will be (1) one siren
At the end of the heat there will be (2) two
sirens, with a 2 second gap between each siren
The commentator must give a five second
countdown at the end of the heat. The first of
two sirens must sound immediately when the
commentator reaches zero. The end of the heat
stops exactly at the start of the first siren.

## Flags

Green flag or disc indicates the heat is in
progress
Yellow flag or disc indicates five minutes remain
in the heat
Red flag or disc in the neutral position indicates
the heat is over
The official timing is done by the Head judge. The
siren is to be located close to the judging podium.
There may be variations in perception of the siren
sounding to indicate the end of a heat and when a
wave was caught as sound travels slower than light.

Generally there is a 30 second break between
heats. Heats are 20 to 30 minutes and finals are
25 to 35 minutes in duration. This is depending on
the conditions and at the discretion of the Head
Judge.

## Priority System

The priority system can be utilised at any normal
competition with 2, 3 or 4 person heats.
Priority is displayed by a coloured and numbered
panel on the judging tower
Priority is allocated by the Priority Judge.

## Competitor’S Communication Signals

If any surfer requires information from the water during a heat they must use hand signals
as follows:

Priority

Wave Count

If the surfer hears and understand any of the above they should acknowledge by waving one arm.

Time Remaining

Score Situation
