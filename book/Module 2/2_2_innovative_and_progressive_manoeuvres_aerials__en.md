## Innovative And Progressive Manoeuvres - Aerials

The classification of all aerials will determine the
degree of difficulty and what are the technical
features of the manoeuvre.
These are generally categorised as technical
(average to good scoring range) and very technical
(high good to excellent scoring range)
Every judge should assess each aerial manoeuvre
using the below descriptions and consider the
degree of difficulty of the following key points:

Where on the wave was the aerial
performed?
Performing aerials on the critical section and off
the lip line is critical to the scoring.
Aerials performed on the face (chop hops) or off
the foam sections do not have a high value and are
seen as the easiest aerials to perform.

How high was the aerial?
The height of the aerial is crucial to the scoring,
Aerials such as straight airs (without any technical
grabs or rotations) can score very well and is
all about how high. Aerials that have a technical
feature but with little height usually do not score in
the good or excellent categories.

Were there any technical aspects?
Technical aspects are classified as rail grabs and
or rotation.
Different rail grabs have different levels of difficulty,
the hardest rail grabs are usually when the surfer is
rotating and grabbing the rail whilst the surfboard
is rotating the opposite way. E.G. (Indy and Stale
Fish)

Did the surfer land cleanly with control?
Regardless of what sort of aerial is performed the
surfer must land cleanly with control and speed.
Showing the judges that the surfer has complete
control after landing is essential to scoring well. If
a surfer can transition into another manoeuvre with
speed clearly demonstrates this aspect.
Standard front side:
This air can be performed by grabbing the toe side
rail with your back arm or without grabbing the rail
at all. Backside airs are similar except you grab
your heel side rail with your front arm.
A variation on the standard front side air is the
double grab (or cannon ball). It should be noted
that back side double grabs are much harder to
perform cleanly.
These airs are the most common and are often
performed with good height.
Classification can be technical and dependent on
height and landing.

Air Reverse: Rotation
An air reverse is when the surfer is traveling
right and launches into the air rotating at least
180-degrees in an anti-clockwise direction. When
the surfer is traveling left the rotation will be
clockwise.

## Rail Grab Aerials

To be done cleanly a grab should commence once a surfer is in the air and be let go of before landing.
Holding on to the rail throughout landing is safer and less clean than releasing in the air.
Doing various grabs (as below) while performing aerials while incorporating spins can be extremely
difficult and should be considered as a higher degree of difficulty.

Alley-oop: Rotation
An Alley-oop is when the surfer is traveling
right and launches into the air rotating at least
180-degrees in a clockwise direction. When the
surfer is traveling left the rotation will be anti-
clockwise.
As with the Air-reverse look for further rotations
past 180-degrees.

## Combination Airs

Combination airs are usually a mix of both rotation
and or rail grabs.
They do have a basis in other sports such as
skateboarding and snowboarding and have been
adapted to the sport of surfing.

Shove-its/Varial:
A shove-it can be done mid face or above the lip.
Involves spinning the board at least 180 degrees
beneath the surfer without changing stance and
will see the surfer ride out fakie (switch foot).To do
this without using your hands to rotate the board
is a Shove it. A Varial is when your leading hand
helps with the rotation of the board beneath the
feet.
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

Big Spin:
Similar to a shove-it except the surfer’s board
spins 360-degrees underneath their feet, while the
surfer spins 180 degrees causing them to land in
a switch foot stance. A big spin has a high degree
of difficulty and very technical.

Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

Passion pop:
Performed front side and involves a surfer
launching their board into double grab alley-oop
motion, flipping their legs off the board (similar to a
superman) and performing a sex change (reversing
their stance to land switch foot).
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

Superman:
Involves a surfer grabbing both rails of the board
in the air, flipping their feet of the board (to fly like
superman) then back onto the board for a clean
landing without hands on the rails.
Classification: Technical with an medium degree of
difficulty.

Christ air:
Similar to a superman but holding the board in one
hand while both hands are perpendicular to the
body then back onto the board for a clean landing.
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.
Judo air:
A backside aerial where the heel-side rail is
grabbed up towards the nose at this time you kick
the front foot out forward before landing with it
back on the board.
Classification: Technical with an high degree of
difficulty.

## Diagonally And

VERTICALLY ROTATED FLIPS.

Gorkin Flip:
A Gorkin flip can only be performed back side and
involves a surfer completing an inverted flip. Similar
to a rodeo clown but lacks the 180-degree spin on
landing.
A Gorkin flip is incredibly difficult and very
technical and innovative.
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

Club Sandwich:
The club sandwich has been classified as an
inverted air reverse under the lip. The surfer usually
goes over the falls with the lip inside of the tubing
section. A club sandwich is incredibly difficult and
very technical and innovative.
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

Kerrupt Flip:
A Kerrupt flip involves a surfer launching into a
double grab alley-oop like rotation, with their front
arm grabbing their toe side rail and back hand
grabbing the heel side rail. This pulls the surfer into
an inverted flip. A Kerrupt flip is incredibly difficult
and very technical and innovative.
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

Rodeo Clown:
A rodeo clown can only be performed back side
and involves a surfer completing an inverted flip
and 360-degree spin. Incredibly difficult and is
extremely technical and innovative.
Classification: Very technical with an high degree
of difficulty.

## Judging Tip

Every judge should assess each aerial
manoeuvre using the below descriptions and
degree of difficulty and the following criteria:
• Where on the wave was the aerial performed?
• How high was the aerial?
• Was there any technical aspects? Such as,
rail grabs and/or rotation
• Did the surfer land cleanly with control and
ride out of the manoeuvre?
