## Stand-Up Paddle Boarding

It is the responsibility of every Contest Director,
Head Judge and Judges is to promote the
below ideas and criteria and to ensure all
competitors develop a standard approach and
understanding of what is expected from an elite
performer in SUP.

## SUP Judging Criteria

Commitment and Degree of Difficulty
Innovative and Progressive manoeuvres
Combination of Major manoeuvres
Variety of manoeuvres
Speed, Power and Flow

The SUP Surfer must perform manoeuvres using
the paddle as a functional tool to maximise the
intensity these manoeuvres.

SUP is unique and it is important to set the criteria
and scoring guidelines apart from other established
surfing disciplines.
When SUP competition began it was common to
see competitors catch the wave using the paddle
but not utilise the paddle in any practical way to
enhance the performance. The paddle is a tool and
a big part of the sport it is not just used to help
catch a wave but is a functional tool for riding the
waves and maximising the intensity of manoeuvres.
The paddle is used in turns as a brace, a pivot, and
a force multiplier. A surfer will be scored higher
when he uses the paddle in some, or all, of these
three ways to achieve sharper or more powerful
turns
Techniques and the use of the paddle allows large
SUP boards to be turned with high rates of speed
and power, stand-up paddle surfing is deemed to
be, at the competition level, a high-performance
discipline of surfing.

## SUP Scoring Guidelines

The judging scale will be the normal 10 point
scale with normal ISA/SA contest rules applying.
The surfer must have forward momentum under
the wave generated power, to be deemed a
scoring ride
To the best of a rider’s ability he/she is always
expected to stand up on the board throughout
the heat, unless the situation of safety dictates
otherwise. Judges will monitor all contestants
and where this rule is not being respected they
(in majority) will instruct the beach announcer to
deliver their first and only warning. If the situation
continues or a second situation arises during
the heat the judges can score a non-priority
interference
Entry into the wave should be by paddling in the
standing position to enable the maximum score
Use of the paddle on the inside rail or the
outside rail will have no difference on the degree
of difficulty of the manoeuvre
Tricks such as twirling or otherwise using the
paddle in a non-functional manner willearn no
extra score for the surfer
With 10-foot SUP competitions major manoeuvres
as well as footwork and nose riding will be scored,
and this will be done in regard to the key words of
the criteria with degree of difficulty and the critical
nature of board positioning.
It is interesting to note that competitors in this
division are emulating elements of modern

longboarding with major manoeuvres as well
as including aspects of these manoeuvres with
traditional variations.
The paddle is used in all manoeuvres as a brace,
a pivot, and a force multiplier. A surfer will be
scored higher when he uses the paddle in some,
or all, of these three ways to achieve control and
commitment to these manoeuvres.
For clear reference on these types of manoeuvres
please refer to the Longboard module in this
manual.
