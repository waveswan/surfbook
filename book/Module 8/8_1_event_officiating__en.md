# Event Officiating

## Event Co-Coordinator

To create and control the event mood as decided
on by the event sponsors, ensure that everyone
is contributing towards the event and doing their
assigned tasks. Reports directly to the event
sponsors and liaises with the national association.

## Contest/Technical Director

To ensure the surfing aspect of the event runs on
schedule and according to the rules and that staff
are not making any errors. Prepares the event
format and daily schedules. The contest director
reports to the Event Co-coordinator and works
with the Tour Representative and Head Judge.
The contest Director makes the daily call on the
venue if it is a mobile event and posts daily event
schedule and technical information on the official
notice board.

## Tour Representative

To aid the Contest Director in rule definitions,
provide the correct seeding for each round of
competition, arbitrate any major problems between
the surfers and the event and provide some
biographical information for the event media liaison
people. Works closely with the Contest Director
and Media Liaison Officer and reports to the Chief
Executive officer of the association and the board.

## Media Liaison

To use the information collected locally, along
with the information provided by the event Media
Liaison Officer and works with the Association
and Tour Representative to create as much local
media interest as possible. Reports to the Event
Coordinator.

## Beach Commentators

The Head Beach Commentator’s primary job is to
bring the event concept to the spectators in an
entertaining and instructive fashion and to lead
the Assistant Commentator in getting the surfing
information from the bio sheets and computer
scoring system to the spectators and surfers. The
Head announcer reports to the Event Coordinator
and Contest Director and works with the Tour
Representative for tour information.

## Assistant Commentator

Works on the computer where applicable and
keeps scoring and biographical information
coming, on cue with the Head Announcer and
reports to the Head announcer.

## Head Judge

Assembles the group of local judges for training
if needed, operates the Head Judges computer
terminal, positions the priority buoy. The Head
Judge cannot overrule the judging panel’s decision
concerning interferences, unless the majority of
judges did not see the incident.
Reports to the Event Coordinator Contest Director
and works with the Regional Judging Coordinators
National Judging manager.
The judging panel usually consists of experienced
judges and one developing judge, coordinated by
Head Judge. The Judging panel in consultation with
the Head judge, establishes the interpretations
of both the Interference Rules and the Judging
Criteria.

## International Judging Panel

The International judging panel usually consists
of several travelling judges, coordinated by the
International Judging Committee and Head Judge.
The Judging panel establishes the interpretations
of both the Interference Rules and the Judging
Criteria. They provide consistency in the judging
decisions from event to event. International Judges
report to the International Head Judge.

## Local Judging Panel

Local Judges are selected by the Regional Judging
Coordinator and Head Judge as the best approved
local talent to be considered for future inclusion on
the National Judging Register and for International
Judging opportunities. Reports to the Head Judge
and receives assistance from the International
Judges.

## Computer Operator

Appointed by the Association and provides
a complete computer scoring system that is
designed for the judging panel. The Computer
Operator supplies all hardware and software. TV
monitors for competitors, media, VIP and other
areas are supplied by the event.

## Replay Operator

The replay operator works closely with the scoring
program/computer operator and captures and logs the
replays in order of the rides being caught. This footage
is used for replays and evaluation by the judges.

## Replay Cameraman

The replay cameraman films each wave of the surfer
from take-off to the end of the ride. This footage is
to be utilised by the replay operator and the judges.

## Timing Disc Operators & Spotters

Work on a rotation basis, calling colours and
operating priority and timing discs. Reports to
the Contest Director and gets directions from the
Head Judge.

## Beach Marshall (Must Be English

## Speaking)

The Beach Marshall ensures that all competitors
are checked-in for their heats, have the correct vest
colours and are briefed with the event rules.
The Head judge will provide a Rule Book to the
Beach Marshall, together with instructions and the
following information:
Heat schedule
Number of scoring waves
Maximum number of waves
A description of disc colours and horn blasts
(i.e. timing discs/flags will be green until 5
minutes remaining)
An explanation of the priority rule
Disc location
When to paddle out and where to wait for the
start of the heat
The Beach Marshall reports to the Contest Director
and receives instructions on the Judging Criteria..

## Tabulator

In all manually judged heats, the Tabulator receives
the score sheets from the Judging Panel and
tabulates them in accordance with the rules. In
computer scored heats the Tabulator collects the
manual heat sheets from the judges but the results
are then only used as a check against the official
computer results.
Reports to the Contest Director and is assisted by
the Tour Representative.
1. In heats that the computer is used, the Tabulator
must check that the judges’ scores have been
entered correctly.
2. In non-computer judged heats, the Tabulator
must do the following:
Check the wave count
Check for any missed waves. (If either of
these occurs, call the Head Judge or Tour
Representative for assistance
Check for interference. If there is a majority,
then it counts and any judge’s sheet that
does not have it marked must have a triangle
placed on that wave
Circle or highlight scoring waves
Tally scoring waves (see Rule Book)
Give placings
If applicable, transpose placings onto master
sheets (lowest place points equaling the top
place competitor. (If there is a tie, see
Rule Book)
Always try to use different coloured ink to the
Judges, if possible.

## Assistant Tabulator

The Assistant tabulator works with the Tabulator
to speed manual calculation of results and the
checking of the competitors’ results. The assistant
must check the tabulation in the same order, with
emphasis on all placings and calculations. Most
mistakes occur because one of the surfer’s best
waves is missed. Whenever a mistake is found and
the adjustment is made, ensure the placings are
changed if necessary. Always try to use different
coloured ink to the judges.
The assistant tabulator reports to the Tabulator.

## Work Crew

Event work crew set-up and take down the event
site it involves placement of event structures such
as tents, signage, general maintenance and all
safety aspects throughout the day.
Safety of the event staff, competitors and the
public is front of mind for event staff.
The layout of the contest area and sponsorship
signage should be placed to maximise exposure.
Walkways, tracks and car parks should be used to
brand and introduce spectators to the contest site.
On the beach, the signage should flow and center
on the major sponsors. With the use of digital
media, video content and photos are instantly
posted on social media sites so it’s crucial the
work crew maintain the event site to the highest
standards at all time.
The work crew reports to the Contest Director and
Event Director.

## Security

To keep the competition and official areas free
from unauthorized entry by non-competitors and
spectators and to keep the site secure overnight.
Security reports to the Contest Director.
